## Core Goal

We are not trying to generate isolated “good images.”

We are trying to create:
- an emotionally coherent comic
- grounded in oral history and memory
- with strong pacing, atmosphere, and thematic continuity

The project should feel:
- human
- intimate
- historically textured
- reflective rather than sensationalized

---

# Guiding Principles

## 1. Story First, Images Second

The narrative spine matters more than visual polish.

Before refining art, we should understand:
- pacing
- emotional escalation
- transitions
- recurring motifs
- thematic coherence

A rough but emotionally effective comic is more valuable than a polished but emotionally flat one.

---

## 2. Build the Whole Thing Early

We should avoid polishing individual pages too early.

Instead:
1. create rough versions of the entire comic
2. read the comic end-to-end
3. identify structural issues
4. revise
5. only then polish visuals

This functions like an animatic or rough cut in filmmaking.

---

## 3. Work at the Page Level

The unit of iteration is:
- a page
- not a single image

Each page should contain:
- panel descriptions
- narration/dialogue
- emotional intent
- visual composition goals

We optimize for readability and flow first.

---

## 4. Embrace Memory-Like Imperfection

Current image models struggle with:
- exact face consistency
- recurring architecture
- strict continuity

This project can use that creatively.

The comic is fundamentally about:
- memory
- oral history
- reconstruction
- inherited experience

Slight visual drift may actually strengthen the feeling of remembered history.

We should prioritize:
- emotional consistency
over
- photographic consistency

---

# Recommended Workflow

## Phase 1 — Storyboarding

Create:
- act structure
- page-by-page outline
- rough panel layouts
- narration/dialogue
- recurring imagery/themes

Focus on:
- emotional clarity
- pacing
- narrative momentum

---

## Phase 2 — Rough Comic Draft

Generate rough versions of all pages.

Goals:
- readable
- emotionally coherent
- visually consistent enough
- fast iteration

Avoid:
- perfectionism
- excessive detail
- over-rendering

This draft exists so we can react to the comic as a whole.

---

## Phase 3 — Structural Revision

After reading the rough draft:
- tighten pacing
- cut redundant scenes
- strengthen transitions
- elevate symbolic moments
- improve emotional rhythm

Potential recurring motifs:
- rain
- walls/gates
- roofs
- rail lines
- ponds/water
- thresholds/borders
- games/childhood
- crowds vs domestic interiors

---

## Phase 4 — Visual Refinement

Only after structure stabilizes:
- improve character consistency
- refine environments
- unify palette/style
- enhance cinematic framing
- improve facial expressions and silence

At this point we can selectively regenerate stronger versions of key pages.

---

# Historical vs Cinematic Truth

The goal is not literal documentary reconstruction.

The goal is:
- emotional truth
- historical atmosphere
- coherent lived experience

Events may be:
- compressed
- merged
- rearranged
- symbolically emphasized

Some moments should function as emotional anchors even if they were one event among many.

---

# Tone Targets

The comic should feel:
- reflective rather than melodramatic
- intimate rather than epic
- grounded rather than mythologized
- humane across communities
- visually warm even amid violence

Important:
ordinary life should remain visible throughout the story.

Games, food, rain, school, family routines, and humor are essential counterweights to the Partition material.

---

# Practical AI Constraints

Current strengths:
- mood
- composition
- painterly atmosphere
- cinematic framing
- rapid iteration

Current weaknesses:
- exact continuity
- precise layout control
- long-range consistency

So:
- iterate quickly
- keep drafts disposable
- preserve flexibility
- avoid overcommitting early

The comic should evolve through repeated passes rather than attempting perfection immediately.
