Devil's advocate:
- Measurable academic outcomes miss many unmeasurable intangible benefits (eg exposing kids to other cultures)
	- Anecdotally heard that cheating / test prep culture was rampant at TJ in the mid-late 2010s, perhaps this helped reverse that?
	- Reminds me of de-segregation via busing lol
- COVID learning loss could explain that data – kids learned less in HS while school was closed. It would have happened irrespective of the policy change.
- Teaching standards at TJ dropped in 2020-2024, independent of admissions change
- National Merit Semifinalists is not a good gauge of academic performance – other academic outcomes were the same/better (GPA, college admissions, SAT)
- The change didn't actually shift the demographic makeup of TJ that much, so the admissions change can't explain this change
- This is explained by teachers needing time to adjust to teaching a wider variety of kids
