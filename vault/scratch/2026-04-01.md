[Extensive branching appears to be quite rare in the worm](https://chatgpt.com/c/69cd3b23-2568-8333-8c5b-1f72c00292b6). There are a few notable examples:
- PVD – nociception, proposed proprioception/stretch sensing
- FLP – nociception, harsh nose touch, thermonociception
- Smaller branching for a few motor neurons (VC4, HSN)
- Others that branch during certain developmental stages but revert
- Hmm also
	- DD motor neurons undergo a well-characterized **synaptic rewiring** during L1→L2 that changes synaptic polarity/partners while the overall morphology remains stereotyped, illustrating that “compartment” can be functional (synaptic domain relocation) rather than grossly structural.

> morphology-centered interpretation: C. elegans achieves much of its functional complexity through **synapse placement, synapse type (chemical vs electrical), and synaptic remodeling** on a relatively stable, compact process scaffold, rather than through large-scale changes in gross neuron branching for most neuron classes
