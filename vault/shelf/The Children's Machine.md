A wonderful book, packed with insightful ideas on both how computers can be used to transform education and how they permit different "ways of knowing" (epistemologies) beyond the abstract that is currently supervalued by school.

Rather than the typical edtech ideas that boil down to "use computers to accelerate what already happens in school", this book provides a jumping-off point for what real transformation might look like. This includes things like:
- Concrete examples of computers & programming empowering students
- Supporting different epistemologies beyond just the abstract/formal – eg learning geometrical ideas by trying to draw your name by programming a physical turtle
- An extension of constructivism called "constructionism" – the role of concrete constructions in the world as a support for those in the head
- Self-directed & *personal* learning – eg designing activities to *give* children the power to expand what their existing culture already values (drawing) rather than designing activities to *get them* to do some specific thing the teacher wants them to learn
- Consideration for sociology & culture as the key force by which such change might actually happen – a suggestion for more experimental Little Schools with a clear educational philosophy
- A word for learning & getting better at it – *mathetics*
