Browsing research Discords (independent science, Eleuther AI, etc.) triggers a comparison spiral: seeing people with established research programs, published articles, and ongoing projects makes my own progress feel inadequate. The spiral goes: I'm behind → what can I add? → maybe I need a PhD → I can't get into a PhD → I don't have enough time → imposter syndrome compounds.

This is structurally identical to social media doom scrolling but with a "productive" veneer. The content feels relevant (it *is* my field), but the mode of engagement — passive scrolling and comparing — is purely destructive.

The antidote is probably the same as regular doom scrolling: don't engage passively. Either participate actively (post, ask questions, share work) or don't open it at all.
