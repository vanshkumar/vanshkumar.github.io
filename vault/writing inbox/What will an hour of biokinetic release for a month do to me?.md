# Biokinetic Release / Rivering — Solo Routine (~60 min)

Reconstructed from Drew Schorno's _The Game of You_, plus the underlying lineage: David MacMurray Smith's Biokinetic Release (built on Wilhelm Reich's body-armor model) leading into Jerzy Grotowski's rivering. No published protocol exists; this is a starting recipe, not the canonical method. Normally done with a trained facilitator present.

## Safety (read before every session, at least at first)

- **You have no facilitator.** Your "ringmaster" mask must stay partially online throughout — you are not aiming for full trance.
- **The injury model applies.** Numbness, lockdown, or fear that spikes rather than flows = the edge. Back off. Do not excavate solo.
- **Exit protocol (decide while calm):** open eyes → name five objects in the room aloud → stand up.
- History of trauma, dissociation, or panic → do this with a practitioner, not alone.

## Setup — 5 min

- Clear floor space with room to stand and move a few steps in any direction.
- Warm room, phone off, door closed, no mirrors facing you, nobody walking in.
- Set a gentle alarm at 45 min as the ringmaster's backstop.
- Rehearse the exit protocol once in your head.

## Phase 1 — Settling and dampening — 5 min

- Lie on your back, knees bent or legs long, eyes closed.
- One slow attention pass, feet to scalp. Notice; don't fix.
- Key instruction: **stop initiating.** Don't move on purpose, don't hold still on purpose — both are intentions.
- Listen for nervous-system noise: micro-twitches, urges, flickers.

## Phase 2 — Biokinetic release: limbs and trunk — 12–15 min

- When an urge appears (finger wants to curl, shoulder wants to roll), **amplify slightly and let it run.** Follow, don't choreograph.
- Calibration test: movement should feel ~70% happening to you, ~30% permitted by you. If it feels performed, shrink back to a twitch and wait.
- Work periphery → center: hands/feet → arms/legs → pelvis/spine.
- Let movements chain: squirm → roll → arch.
- It will look stupid. The "observing and judging" mask arising is material — note it, don't obey it.

## Phase 3 — Breath, throat, face — 8–10 min

The Reichian core; where the emotional voltage lives.

- Bring attention to the diaphragm. **Break the pendulum of the breath:** irregular rhythms, staccato exhales, held-then-released inhales.
- Let it become sound — sighs, huffs, squeaks, nonsense syllables. Let the face contort with it.
- The irregular pattern will get _interpreted_ — as laughter, sobbing, or both in sequence. Let each come fully.
- **Don't hold onto any state.** No manufacturing reasons to keep laughing; no clinging to grief. Practice letting affect move through without narrative capture.
- Numbness or rigidity surfacing = stop signal for this phase. Return to ordinary breathing.

## Phase 4 — Rivering — 12–15 min

- The narrator ("Mickey") has been generating story fragments to explain your movements. **Now follow him.** Paddling motion → you're in a boat → commit to the boat.
- Speak in full sentences, to imaginary characters.
- Let the fantasy pull you off the floor: kneeling, standing, walking, running a few steps.
- Change masks freely and without loyalty — tightrope walker one minute, the wind the next.
- Two rules:
    1. Keep the ringmaster question faintly running: _is this safe, here, now?_ (furniture, volume, neighbors)
    2. Distress that still feels like a _game_ is workable. Distress that doesn't → exit protocol.

## Phase 5 — Return — 8–10 min

- Back to the floor. Two minutes of ordinary, regular breathing — restore the pendulum.
- Open eyes, sit up slowly, look around and name what you see.
- **Journal 5 min while fresh:** which masks showed up, what the body did that surprised you, any emotion that arrived without a story attached. This is the capture step; skip it and the session evaporates like a dream.

## Frequency and expectations

- Schorno's cohort: daily for months, supervised — that's where large changes came from.
- Solo: 2–3×/week to start.
- First several sessions will be mostly awkward twitching and self-consciousness. The dampening skill (Phase 2) is the bottleneck for everything downstream.
- Mechanistic framing (lowered top-down motor priors, amplified bottom-up noise, real-time confabulation by the interpreter) is a useful anchor if things get intense — but drop it during practice. Analyzing while rivering is just another judge mask.
