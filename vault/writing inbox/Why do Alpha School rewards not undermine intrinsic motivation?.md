First, is the premise even accurate? Do Alpha graduates have intrinsic motivation for *formal academics*? Selection effects dominate again...

Separately, is intrinsic motivation for formal academics valuable? Lmao. I suppose it's more intrinsic motivation for learning that's useful..

But the core question here is that [[If you already like doing something, receiving external rewards decreases intrinsic motivation]]. Why does Alpha not seem to suffer from this, assuming the premise is true?

My hypothesis is that it is because they are very personalized rewards chosen by guides who know the kids well and based on mastery. Plus there is no comparison to others because each child is going at their own pace?

Another way to say this – if extrinsic rewards are given to you by someone who you love and you know they love you & want the best for you. It's believable to me that that doesn't undermine motivation (or at least not nearly as much). Is this tested in the literature or is it all lab studies by random third party researchers?

Why might this make sense? Ultimately all rewards are "intrinsic." Even if someone puts a gun to your head to make you do something, the motivation for doing that thing is internal – *you* don't want to die. So if the reward is always intrinsic, the difference has to boil down to your model of the world / the other person.
