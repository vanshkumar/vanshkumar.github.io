A single neuron with a dendritic tree can reportedly solve MNIST. This is surprising if you hold the standard point-neuron model where a neuron is just a weighted sum + nonlinearity.

If dendritic trees enable individual neurons to perform complex nonlinear computations, this has implications for:
- [[The computational innovation of neurons was architectural]] — the innovation may not be *just* architectural (network-level) but also at the single-cell level via dendritic branching
- [[What mathematical language is appropriate for describing brain computation?]] — point-neuron models may be fundamentally wrong, not just incomplete

What is the right mathematical formalization of a neuron that accounts for dendritic computation?
