Any complete theory of how the brain works needs to specify four things:

1. **Initializations** — what does genetics give you at birth? (connectome, ion channels, receptor distributions) → [[How does DNA bridge the micro (local neuronal rules) to the macro (evolutionarily relevant behavior)?]]
2. **Cost function** — what is the brain optimizing for? (behavior? prediction error? survival?) → [[Natural selection acts at the level of behavior, not individual neurons]]
3. **Plasticity** — what structural/functional changes are possible?
4. **Learning rules** — what local rules govern how neurons change?

These four map roughly onto the ML decomposition of: architecture, loss function, optimizer, and training dynamics.
