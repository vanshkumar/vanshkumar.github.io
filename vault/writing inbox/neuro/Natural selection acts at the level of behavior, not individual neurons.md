Open question: does evolution care about individual neurons *at all*? DNA specifies local rules (ion channels, receptor types, connectivity patterns), but selection pressure operates on behavior. So in what sense does a gene "see" a neuron? It sees a behavioral outcome that a neuron contributes to — but the same behavior can be implemented by different neural configurations ([[Each brain has its own neural coordinate system to implement the same behavior]]). This degeneracy suggests evolution cares about the computational constraint, not the specific wiring.

But at the same time, it is ultimately scale & specific wiring that distinguishes species.
