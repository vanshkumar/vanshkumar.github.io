Not that we need more data with existing methods, but that we need better measurement tools & methods. Theories of the brain have shifted from a single-neuron view to global population dynamics due to advancements in measurement technology in the last ~15-20 years.

This is basically Adam Marblestone's thesis in the Gap Map.



The single neuron doctrine is that looking at the firing rates of a single neuron is informative. This is more true the closer you are to the outside world (eg retinal neuron), but much less true deeper in the brain. Neuronal population dynamics appear to be more informative deeper in the brain, which we only figured out because of advances in whole-brain recording technology.
