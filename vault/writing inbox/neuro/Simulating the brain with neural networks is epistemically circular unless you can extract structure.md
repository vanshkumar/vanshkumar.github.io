If you train a neural network to simulate brain dynamics, you haven't explained anything — you've just moved the mystery from one black box to another. We can't interpret artificial neural networks either.

But: if the simulation reveals shared computational structure (e.g., LOOPER finding overlapping low-dimensional dynamics across worms), then the simulation *is* useful — not as an explanation, but as a tool for discovering structure that you can then formalize.

Can you always extract interpretable structure from a neural simulation of the brain, or only sometimes? What determines when this works?
