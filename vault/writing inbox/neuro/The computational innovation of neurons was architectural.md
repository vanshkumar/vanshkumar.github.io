[[A single neuron is a stateful nonlinear dynamical system]], and so are many unicellular protein-signaling networks. Excitation, inhibition, thresholding, adaptation, and temporal signal integration all predated neurons in intracellular biochemical networks; for example, bacterial chemotaxis implements robust perfect adaptation via integral feedback control.

What neurons — more precisely, synaptically connected excitable cells in multicellular nervous systems — added was fast spatially distributed circuit architecture: cell-to-cell recurrent circuits, lateral inhibition, coincidence detection across spatially separated inputs, and synapse-specific activity-dependent plasticity.
